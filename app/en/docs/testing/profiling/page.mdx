## Measure → hypothesize → fix → re-measure

A four-step loop instead of guesswork. Change exactly one thing per iteration.

## Renderer: DevTools Performance

1. Open DevTools → Performance tab.
2. Press the red ⬤ Record, run the scenario.
3. Stop. In the _Main Thread_ swim-lane, look at the widest yellow/orange bars first.

<EasyNote>
One fat bar usually causes _90% of the slowness_. Chase the big bar before the hundred small ones.
</EasyNote>

## Main: Node --prof

```bash filename="terminal"
electron --prof --enable-logging .
# After quitting you'll have isolate-*.log

node --prof-process isolate-*.log > prof.txt
```

`prof.txt` has cumulative time per function.

## Memory profile

DevTools _Memory_ → Heap Snapshot. Follow the _Retainers_ tree to find objects that are still referenced when they shouldn't be.

## In plain words

Saying "the app is slow" without profiling is like walking into a doctor's office and only saying "I'm sick." The moment you _guess_ without measuring, you'll almost always fix the wrong thing. A profiler is the stethoscope and the X-ray combined — it tells you _where_ the problem really lives, in numbers.

For the Renderer, the DevTools _Performance_ tab is enough by itself. Hit the red ⬤ Record button, run the scenario, stop. You'll get a mountain of bars — and one _wide yellow or orange bar_ usually accounts for 70–90% of the perceived slowness. Don't chase a hundred small bars; chase the one fat one.

When Main itself is the problem, launch with `electron --prof` to enable _Node's V8 profiler_, then process the resulting `isolate-*.log` with `node --prof-process` for a per-function time breakdown. For memory leaks, two _Heap Snapshots_ taken minutes apart and a walk through the _Retainers_ tree of the growing objects almost always reveals the culprit.
