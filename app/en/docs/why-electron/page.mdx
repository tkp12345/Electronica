## One codebase, three operating systems

"Make it run on Windows, macOS, and Linux" is the most common request you'll hear from users. Instead of learning Swift, C#, and GTK and writing three separate apps, Electron lets you ship **one web codebase to all three**. That's the headline win.

<Callout type="info" emoji="🌍">
Think "one bicycle that can ride to three cities" rather than "three different driving tests for three different cities."
</Callout>

## What you gain

- **Speed of development**: HTML/CSS/JS as-is — zero new languages to learn.
- **Reuse**: any design system or component library your team already has carries over.
- **Ecosystem**: hundreds of thousands of npm packages, all available.
- **Desktop primitives** built in: auto-update, tray, menus, global shortcuts.
- **DevTools**: the full Chrome DevTools you know, attached to your own app.

## What you give up

- **Size on disk and in RAM**: the bundled Chromium runtime is heavy — usually 50–150MB on disk.
- **Startup time**: slower than native; first paint sits in the 100–300ms range.
- **Deep OS integration**: if you truly need native widgets, you'll hit limits.
- **Security responsibility**: an Electron app has more power than a browser tab, so you have to think about it carefully (→ [Security](/en/docs/best-practices/security)).

## How it compares

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Electron** | One codebase, huge ecosystem | Heavy |
| **Tauri** | Tiny binaries (system webview) | Webview differs per OS, weaker tooling |
| **Native (Swift/Kotlin/...)** | Fastest and lightest | Build three times |
| **React Native Desktop** | Reuse RN ecosystem | Desktop story still maturing |

## When Electron is the right call

- You already have a web app and need a _desktop_ version (the natural case).
- You must ship to all three OSes with limited engineering capacity.
- You're building a tooling app — editor, chat, design surface — where rich UI and fast iteration matter most.

<Callout type="warning">
If "must be as lightweight as possible" is your number-one requirement, look at **Tauri** or native before reaching for Electron.
</Callout>
