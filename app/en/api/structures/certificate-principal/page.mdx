A `CertificatePrincipal` describes who a certificate is about. It appears as the `issuer` and `subject` fields of the [`Certificate`](/en/api/structures/certificate) object, which you receive in events like the `session` module's `certificate-error` and `select-client-certificate`.

## Fields

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `commonName` | `string` | Common Name (CN) of the principal, e.g. the domain or person being identified. |
| `organizations` | `string[]` | Organization names (O) the principal belongs to. |
| `organizationUnits` | `string[]` | Organization Unit names (OU) within the organization. |
| `locality` | `string` | Locality, typically the city. |
| `state` | `string` | State or province. |
| `country` | `string` | Country or region. |

```js
const subject = {
  commonName: 'example.com',
  organizations: ['Example Inc.'],
  organizationUnits: ['Engineering'],
  locality: 'San Francisco',
  state: 'California',
  country: 'US',
}
```

<EasyNote>
Think of it as the "address block" on an ID card: it spells out exactly who the certificate belongs to and where they are.
</EasyNote>

## In plain words

When your app inspects a website's TLS certificate, you often want to know who issued it and who it was issued to. `CertificatePrincipal` is that "who" answer, broken into clean fields like name, organization, and location. You'd read it to display certificate details or to decide whether to trust a connection.
